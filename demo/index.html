Login

Sign in without an account.

No email. No password. No form to fill. Your wallet sends a zero-value shielded transaction carrying a one-time code, Portal watches it arrive on the Zcash mainnet, and you are in.

The site you signed into learns exactly one thing: that you showed up. Not who you are, not where you came from, not what else you have paid for.

1

A one-time code appears on screen, as a QR or a tap-to-pay link.

2

Your wallet sends it back inside an encrypted memo. It costs nothing beyond the network fee.

3

Portal sees it land on mainnet, usually within seconds. Signed in.

Gates

Put a price on the door.

A Gate is a login with a price. Set an amount, and the exact same one-step flow becomes a paywall, a membership, a ticket booth. No billing system, no checkout form, no card processor taking a cut.

The price lives on your server, never in the visitor's browser, so nobody can talk it down. Underpay and the door stays shut, with an honest message saying exactly why.

Paylinks

Get paid with a link.

Choose an amount, add a label, share the link. Anyone who opens it can pay you straight from their wallet, and Portal confirms each payment live on mainnet.

A tip jar, an invoice, a buy-me-a-coffee button. Many people can pay the same link, each detected on its own, and a running count keeps score.

The Shielded Wall

Proof, written through the chain.

Add a few words after the code in a login memo and they appear on the Wall, published from inside an encrypted transaction. No account posted it. No IP address sent it.

Look the transaction up on a public block explorer and you will find nothing: sender, receiver, amount, and message are all sealed. That silence is the product.
